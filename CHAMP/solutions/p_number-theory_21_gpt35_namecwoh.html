Modular arithmetic is a system of arithmetic that deals with remainders. It is often used in number theory, cryptography, and computer science. The basic idea is to perform arithmetic operations on numbers within a fixed range, called the modulus.

In modular arithmetic, we consider a set of numbers that are congruent to each other modulo a given modulus. Two numbers are said to be congruent modulo n if their difference is divisible by n. This is denoted as a ≡ b (mod n), where a and b are the numbers being compared, and n is the modulus.

Modular arithmetic follows a set of rules that govern the operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. These rules are:
