1. The first child (on the left end) has 2 options: stay or move right.
2. The last child (on the right end) also has 2 options: stay or move left.

- Case 2: Exactly one child moves. So, we have \( n \) ways for the children to choose who moves and 2 ways for the direction, giving \( n \times 2 \) ways. - Case 3: Two children move, one to the left and one to the right. They can't be adjacent, otherwise, they would end up in the same seat.
