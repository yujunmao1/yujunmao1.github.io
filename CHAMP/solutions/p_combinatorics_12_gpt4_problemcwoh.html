This problem is a classic problem in combinatorics known as the Menage problem or the problem of derangements.

The number of derangements of n elements, denoted by D(n), can be calculated using the formula:

D(n) = n! * (1 - 1/1! + 1/2! - 1/3! + ... + (-1)^n/n!)

This formula is derived from the principle of inclusion-exclusion, which is a counting technique used in combinatorics.
