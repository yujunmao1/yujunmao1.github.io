Let $a^2 = x$, $b^2 = y$, and $c^2 = z$. Then, we want to minimize $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - xy - xz - yz$.

We can write this as $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 2(xy + xz + yz)$.

By AM-GM, we have

\begin{align*}
x^2 + y^2 + z^2 &= (x + y + z)^2 - 2(xy + xz + yz) \\
&\ge 3 \sqrt[3]{(x + y + z)^3} \\
&= 3 \sqrt[3]{(x^2 + y^2 + z^2 + 2(xy + xz + yz))^3} \\

&= 3x^2 + 3y^2 + 3z^2 + 6(xy + xz + yz).
\end{align*}
