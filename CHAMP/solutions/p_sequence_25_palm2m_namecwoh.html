A geometric progression is a sequence of numbers where each term after the first is found by multiplying the previous one by a constant value called the common ratio. For example, in the sequence 2, 6, 18, 54, ..., the common ratio is 3.

The sum of a finite geometric progression is given by the formula

$$S_n = \frac{a_1(1-r^n)}{1-r}$$

where $a_1$ is the first term, $r$ is the common ratio, and $n$ is the number of terms.
