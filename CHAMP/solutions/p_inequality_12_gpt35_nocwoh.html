Let (a_1, a_2, ..., a_n) be a permutation of (1, 2, ..., n). What is the smallest value of sum_(k=1)^n a_k/k^2 as an expression of n?

Let's consider the terms of the sum individually. We have a_k/k^2 for each k from 1 to n. 

To minimize each term, we want to assign the smallest possible value to a_k for each k. Since a_k must be one of the numbers from 1 to n, we assign a_k = k. 

Now, let's substitute these values into the sum:

sum_(k=1)^n a_k/k^2 = sum_(k=1)^n k/k^2 = sum_(k=1)^n 1/k

This is a well-known series called the harmonic series. It is known that the harmonic series diverges, meaning that the sum goes to infinity as n approaches infinity. 

Therefore, there is no smallest value for the sum as an expression of n.
