Let's denote \( n \) positive real numbers by \( a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n \). The arithmetic mean \( A \) and the harmonic mean \( H \) of these numbers are defined as follows:

\[ A = \frac{a_1 + a_2 + \ldots + a_n}{n} \]

\[ H = \frac{n}{\frac{1}{a_1} + \frac{1}{a_2} + \ldots + \frac{1}{a_n}} \]

The inequality of arithmetic and harmonic means states that:
\[ A \geq H \]

\[ G = \sqrt[n]{a_1 \cdot a_2 \cdot \ldots \cdot a_n} \]

The AM-GM inequality is:
\[ A \geq G \]

We also have the GM-HM inequality, which states that the geometric mean is always greater than or equal to the harmonic mean:
\[ G \geq H \]

Combining these two inequalities, we get:
\[ A \geq G \geq H \]

Thus, the inequality of arithmetic and harmonic means for \( n \) variables is established. The equality \( A = H \) occurs if and only if all the \( a_i \)'s are equal, because this is the only case when the AM-GM and GM-HM inequalities both become equalities.
