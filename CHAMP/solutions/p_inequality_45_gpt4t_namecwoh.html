The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality is a fundamental result in linear algebra and analysis, with numerous applications in various fields of mathematics and the sciences. It provides an upper bound for the absolute value of the inner product of two vectors in terms of the product of their norms.

Let's consider a real or complex inner product space, which is a vector space equipped with an inner product. The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality states that for any vectors \( \mathbf{u} \) and \( \mathbf{v} \) in an inner product space, the following inequality holds:

\[ |\langle \mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v} \rangle| \leq \|\mathbf{u}\| \cdot \|\mathbf{v}\| \]

The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality has several important consequences, such as the triangle inequality for norms and the fact that the angle between two vectors in an inner product space is well-defined.
