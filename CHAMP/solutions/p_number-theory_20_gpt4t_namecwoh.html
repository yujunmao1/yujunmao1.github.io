[n choose k] = n! / (k! * (n-k)!)

where "n!" denotes the factorial of n, which is the product of all positive integers up to n (n! = n * (n-1) * (n-2) * ... * 1).

a^k ≡ b^k (mod n)

Division is a bit more complex in modular arithmetic. If a ≡ b (mod n) and c ≡ d (mod n), it is not generally true that a/c ≡ b/d (mod n). a ≡ b (mod n)
