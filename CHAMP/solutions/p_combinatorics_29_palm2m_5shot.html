A diagnoal in a convex polygon is any line connecting two non-adjacent vertices. For a convex polygon of n vertices, we draw all possible diagonals and suppose that no three diagonals pass through the same point. How many such intersection points do we have in the interior of the polygon, as an expression of n?
Let $n$ be the number of vertices in the polygon. For each pair of non-adjacent vertices, there is one diagonal connecting them. There are $\binom{n}{2}$ pairs of non-adjacent vertices, so there are $\binom{n}{2}$ diagonals.

Each diagonal intersects the polygon at two points, one on each side of the diagonal. If two diagonals intersect, they intersect at one point. Thus, the total number of intersection points is $2\binom{n}{2}-\binom{n}{3}$.

The number of intersection points is also equal to the number of ways to choose three vertices from the $n$ vertices, such that no two of the chosen vertices are adjacent. This can be done in $\binom{n}{3}$ ways.
