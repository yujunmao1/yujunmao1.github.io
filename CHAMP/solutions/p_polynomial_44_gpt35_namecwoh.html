Polynomial division is a method used to divide one polynomial by another polynomial. It is similar to long division that we use for dividing numbers, but instead of numbers, we are dividing polynomials.

In polynomial division, we have a dividend, which is the polynomial being divided, and a divisor, which is the polynomial we are dividing by. The goal is to find the quotient and remainder.

The process of polynomial division involves the following steps:

1. Arrange the dividend and divisor in descending order of their degrees. If any term is missing in either polynomial, we can represent it with a coefficient of zero.

2. Divide the highest degree term of the dividend by the highest degree term of the divisor. This gives us the first term of the quotient.

3. Multiply the entire divisor by the first term of the quotient.

4. Subtract the result obtained in step 3 from the dividend.

5. Repeat steps 2-4 with the new dividend obtained in step 4, until we can no longer divide.

6. The final result is the quotient obtained from the division and the remainder, if any.

a^p ≡ a (mod p)
