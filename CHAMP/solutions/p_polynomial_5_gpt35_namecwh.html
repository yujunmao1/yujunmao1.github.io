The process of polynomial division involves the following steps:

1. Arrange the dividend and divisor in descending order of their degrees.

2. Divide the term with the highest degree in the dividend by the term with the highest degree in the divisor. This gives us the first term of the quotient.

3. Multiply the entire divisor by the first term of the quotient.

4. Subtract the result obtained in step 3 from the dividend.

5. Repeat steps 2-4 with the new dividend obtained in step 4, until we can no longer divide.

6. The final result is the quotient obtained from the division, along with any remaining terms, which form the remainder.

The quotient represents how many times the divisor can be divided into the dividend, while the remainder represents any leftover terms that cannot be divided evenly.
