The Monotone Convergence Theorem (MCT) is a fundamental result in real analysis, particularly in the context of measure theory and integration. Here is the statement of the Monotone Convergence Theorem for non-decreasing sequences of functions:

\[

\]

The Monotone Convergence Theorem is particularly useful because it allows us to interchange the limit and the integral, which is not always possible in more general settings. This theorem is a key tool in proving other important results in analysis, such as the Dominated Convergence Theorem and the Fatou's Lemma.

It's important to note that the Monotone Convergence Theorem requires the functions to be non-negative.
