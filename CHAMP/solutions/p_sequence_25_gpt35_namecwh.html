The geometric progression sum formula is used to find the sum of a geometric progression, which is a sequence of numbers where each term is obtained by multiplying the previous term by a constant factor called the common ratio (r).

The formula for the sum of a geometric progression is given by:

S = a * (1 - r^n) / (1 - r)

Where:
- S represents the sum of the geometric progression.
- a is the first term of the sequence.
- r is the common ratio.
- n is the number of terms in the sequence.

To understand how this formula works, let's consider an example. Suppose we have a geometric progression with a first term of 2 and a common ratio of 3. We want to find the sum of the first 5 terms.

Using the formula, we have:
S = 2 * (1 - 3^5) / (1 - 3)
S = 2 * (1 - 243) / (-2)
S = 2 * (-242) / (-2)
S = 242

Therefore, the sum of the first 5 terms of this geometric progression is 242.

It's important to note that the formula only works when the absolute value of the common ratio (|r|) is less than 1. If |r| is greater than or equal to 1, the geometric progression diverges and the sum is infinite.
