The inequality of arithmetic and geometric means (AM-GM inequality) is a fundamental result in algebra that relates the arithmetic mean and the geometric mean of a set of non-negative real numbers. For any set of \( n \) non-negative real numbers \( a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n \), the AM-GM inequality states that the arithmetic mean is always greater than or equal to the geometric mean. That is:

\[

\]

with equality if and only if \( a_1 = a_2 = \cdots = a_n \).

\]

2. **Geometric Mean (GM)**: This is the \( n \)-th root of the product of the numbers. For \( n \) numbers \( a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n \), the geometric mean is:

\[
GM = \sqrt[n]{a_1 \cdot a_2 \cdot \ldots \cdot a_n}
