Please explain the following concept: rearrangement inequality.
The rearrangement inequality is a fundamental result in combinatorial mathematics that provides a way to compare the sums of products of elements from two sequences when the sequences are permuted in different ways. It states that, for any two sequences of real numbers that are both either non-increasing or non-decreasing, the sum of the products of corresponding terms is maximized when the sequences are ordered in the same way and minimized when the sequences are ordered in the opposite way.

More formally, let's consider two sequences of real numbers:

\( a_1 \geq a_2 \geq \ldots \geq a_n \)
\( b_1 \geq b_2 \geq \ldots \geq b_n \)

These sequences are non-increasing. The rearrangement inequality states that for any permutation \( \sigma \) of the indices \( \{1, 2, \ldots, n\} \), the following inequality holds:

\( a_1b_1 + a_2b_2 + \ldots + a_nb_n \geq a_1b_{\sigma(1)} + a_2b_{\sigma(2)} + \ldots + a_nb_{\sigma(n)} \)

And the sum is minimized when the largest element of one sequence is paired with the smallest element of the other sequence, and so on, which corresponds to the permutation that reverses the order of one of the sequences:

\( a_1b_n + a_2b_{n-1} + \ldots + a_nb_1 \leq a_1b_{\sigma(1)} + a_2b_{\sigma(2)} + \ldots + a_nb_{\sigma(n)} \)

The rearrangement inequality also holds if both sequences are non-decreasing. The key point is that the sequences must be ordered in the same direction (either both non-increasing or both non-decreasing) for the inequality to apply.

This inequality has applications in various areas of mathematics, including optimization, algebra, and analysis. It is often used in mathematical competitions and problem-solving to establish bounds or to prove that a certain arrangement of terms is optimal.
