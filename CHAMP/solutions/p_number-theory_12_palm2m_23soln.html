An integer a is called automorphic if a^2 ends in a. For example, 5 is automorphic because 5^2=25 ends in 5. 25 is also automorphic because 25^2=625 ends in 25. Find all two-digit automorphic numbers besides 25.

Below is a partial solution to the problem that may be helpful:

1. We have 10≤a≤99 and since a^2 ends in a, a^2-a ends in 00.
2. So 100 | a(a-1).
3. Since gcd(a, a-1)=1, we have one of a and a-1 being a multiple of 4, and the other being a multiple of 25, subject to 10≤a≤99 (or one being a multiple of 100, but this is impossible because a is a two-digit number).
4. If 25 | a and 4 | a-1, the only solution is a=25, as introduced in the question.
Let $a$ be a two-digit automorphic number besides 25. Then $a^2$ ends in $a$, and $a$ is not divisible by 25. Since $a$ is a two-digit number, $10 \le a \le 99$.

We can write $a^2 = 100k + a$ for some integer $k$. Subtracting $a$ from both sides, we get $a^2 - a - 100k = 0$. This factors as $(a - 5)(a - 20) = 0$. Therefore, either $a = 5$ or $a = 20$.

However, $a = 25$ is not a two-digit number, so the only two-digit automorphic number besides 25 is $a = 5$.

Therefore, the answer is $\boxed{5}$.
