3. C(n, r)=n!/(r!*(n-r)!)=n*(n-1)*(n-2)*...*(n-r+1)/r!.
4. C(n, k)=C(n, n-k).
To solve this problem, we can use combinatorics. We have 8 dogs and 3 identical cookies. We want to distribute these cookies to the dogs such that no two dogs share the same cookie, and all cookies are distributed.

Since the cookies are identical, we can think of this as a problem of partitioning the dogs into groups where each group corresponds to a cookie. We can use the "stars and bars" method (also known as "balls and urns") to solve this problem. In this method, we represent the cookies as "stars" and the divisions between different dogs as "bars".

We have 3 cookies (stars) to distribute among 8 dogs, and we need 2 bars to create 3 sections (one for each cookie). The total number of objects (stars + bars) is 3 + 2 = 5.

Now, we need to arrange these 5 objects in a row. The number of ways to do this is the number of ways to choose 2 positions for the bars out of 5 positions (since the stars are identical, their positions don't matter, only the positions of the bars do).

         = 10
