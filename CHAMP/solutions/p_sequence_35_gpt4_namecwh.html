Let's consider a set of n non-negative real numbers a1, a2, ..., an. The arithmetic mean (AM) of these numbers is calculated as the sum of the numbers divided by the number of elements, i.e., (a1 + a2 + ... + an) / n. The geometric mean (GM) is calculated as the nth root of the product of the numbers, i.e., (a1 * a2 * ... * an)^(1/n).

The AM-GM inequality states that AM ≥ GM. In other words, (a1 + a2 + ... + an) / n ≥ (a1 * a2 * ... * an)^(1/n).

This inequality is widely used in various fields of mathematics, including algebra, calculus, optimization, and number theory.
