Let $n$ be a composite number. We have
\begin{align*}
Q(n) &= \frac{1\cdot 2\cdots (n-2)\cdot (n-1)}{1+2+\cdots+(n-2)+(n-1)} \\
&= \frac{1\cdot 2\cdots (n-2)\cdot (n-1)}{n(n-1)/2} \\
&= \frac{2\cdot 2\cdot 3\cdots (n-2)}{n}.
\end{align*}
Since $n$ is composite, we let $n=pq$, where $1<p\leq q<n$. We further have $q<n-1$ since $\gcd(n,n-1)=1$. Thus, if $p\neq q$, then $p$ and $q$ are two factors less than $n-1$ and thus separately appear among the terms of $2\cdot 3\cdots (n-2)$. Therefore,
\begin{align*}
Q(n) &= \frac{2\cdot 2\cdot 3\cdots (n-2)}{n} \\
&= \frac{2^2\cdot 3\cdots (n-2)}{n} \\
&= \frac{2^2\cdot (n-2)!}{n!} \\
&\equiv \frac{2^2}{n} \pmod{n}.
\end{align*}
