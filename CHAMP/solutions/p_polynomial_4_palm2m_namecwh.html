Please explain the following concept: geometric progression sum formula.
A geometric progression is a sequence of numbers where each term after the first is found by multiplying the previous one by a constant value called the common ratio. For example, in the sequence 2, 6, 18, 54, ..., the common ratio is 3.

The sum of a finite geometric progression is given by the formula

$$S_n = \frac{a_1(1-r^n)}{1-r}$$

where $a_1$ is the first term, $r$ is the common ratio, and $n$ is the number of terms.

To see why this formula works, consider the following diagram:

[Diagram of a geometric progression]

The sum of the areas of the rectangles is equal to the area of the shaded triangle. The area of the triangle is $\frac{1}{2}bh$, where $b$ is the base and $h$ is the height. The base of the triangle is the sum of the first and last terms of the progression, and the height is the common ratio. Therefore, the sum of the geometric progression is

$$S_n = \frac{1}{2}(a_1 + a_nr^n)r = \frac{a_1(1+r^n)}{2}$$

Dividing both sides by $1-r$ gives the formula above.

The geometric progression sum formula can be used to find the sum of any finite geometric progression. For example, to find the sum of the first 5 terms of the progression 2, 6, 18, 54, ..., we would use the formula with $a_1=2$, $r=3$, and $n=5$. This gives

$$S_5 = \frac{2(1-3^5)}{1-3} = -104$$

Therefore, the sum of the first 5 terms of the progression is -104.
