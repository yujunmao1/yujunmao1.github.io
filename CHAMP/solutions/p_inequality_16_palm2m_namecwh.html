The inequality of arithmetic and harmonic means for $n$ variables states that the arithmetic mean of $n$ numbers is always greater than or equal to the harmonic mean of the same numbers. In other words, $\frac{a_1+a_2+\dots+a_n}{n} \geq \frac{1}{n} \left(\frac{1}{a_1} + \frac{1}{a_2} + \dots + \frac{1}{a_n}\right)$.

This inequality can be proven by using the following steps:

1. Let $a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n$ be any $n$ numbers.
2. Define $b_i = \frac{1}{a_i}$ for $i = 1, 2, \dots, n$.
3. Note that $\sum_{i=1}^n b_i = 1$.
4. By the arithmetic-geometric mean inequality, we have $\left(\prod_{i=1}^n a_i\right)^{1/n} \geq \sqrt[n]{\prod_{i=1}^n a_i}$.
5. Taking the reciprocal of both sides, we get $\frac{1}{\left(\prod_{i=1}^n a_i\right)^{1/n}} \leq \frac{1}{\sqrt[n]{\prod_{i=1}^n a_i}}$.
6. Multiplying both sides by $n$, we get $n \leq \frac{n}{\sqrt[n]{\prod_{i=1}^n a_i}} = \sum_{i=1}^n b_i$.
7. Finally, dividing both sides by $n$, we get $\frac{1}{n} \left(\frac{1}{a_1} + \frac{1}{a_2} + \dots + \frac{1}{a_n}\right) \leq 1$.

Therefore, the arithmetic mean of $n$ numbers is always greater than or equal to the harmonic mean of the same numbers.
