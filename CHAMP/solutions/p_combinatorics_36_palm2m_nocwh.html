There are $n!$ ways to arrange $n$ people around a circular table. However, not all of these arrangements are distinct. For example, if we rotate the table by 180 degrees, the arrangement of the people will be the same. Similarly, if we reflect the table through the center, the arrangement of the people will be the same.

Therefore, the number of distinct arrangements of $n$ people around a circular table is the number of ways to arrange them divided by the number of ways to rotate or reflect the table without changing the arrangement. This number is given by the following formula:

$$\frac{n!}{n}$$
