For x_1, x_2, ..., x_n≥0, with x_1+x_2+...+x_n=1, what is the minimum value of x_1^2+x_2^2+...+x_n^2, as an expression of n?
Let $x_1^2+x_2^2+\cdots+x_n^2=S$. Then, we have
\begin{align*}
S &= x_1^2+x_2^2+\cdots+x_n^2 \\\
&= (x_1+x_2+\cdots+x_n)^2-2(x_1x_2+\cdots+x_nx_{n-1})+x_1^2x_2^2+\cdots+x_n^2 \\\
&= 1-2(x_1x_2+\cdots+x_nx_{n-1})+x_1^2x_2^2+\cdots+x_n^2 \\\
&\ge 1-2\sqrt{(x_1x_2+\cdots+x_nx_{n-1})^2}+x_1^2x_2^2+\cdots+x_n^2 \\\
&= 1-2\sqrt{(x_1^2+x_2^2+\cdots+x_n^2)(x_2^2+x_3^2+\cdots+x_n^2)}+x_1^2x_2^2+\cdots+x_n^2 \\\
&= 1-2\sqrt{S(S-x_1^2-x_2^2-\cdots-x_n^2)}+S \\\
&= 1-2\sqrt{S(1-S)}+S \\\
&= \frac{1+S^2-2S}{\sqrt{1-S}} \\\
&= \frac{1+S^2-2S}{\sqrt{1-S^2}} \\\
&= \frac{1+S-\sqrt{S^2-1}}{\sqrt{1-S^2}} \\\
&= \frac{1+S-\sqrt{(S-1)(S+1)}}{\sqrt{1-S^2}} \\\
&= \frac{1+S-(S-1)}{\sqrt{1-S^2}} \\\
&= \frac{2}{\sqrt{1-S^2}}.
\end{align*}Equality occurs when $x_1=x_2=\cdots=x_n=\frac{1}{n}$. Therefore, the minimum value of $S$ is $\frac{4}{n}$.
