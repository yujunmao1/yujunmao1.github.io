However, since a_n ≠ 0, at least one of these coefficients must be non-zero. Therefore, at least one of these coefficients must be odd, because the sum of even numbers is even, so to get an even sum with at least one non-zero number, there must be an odd number.

Now, let's consider the possible integer roots of the polynomial. By the Integer Root Theorem, any integer root r of the polynomial must divide the constant term a_0. But a_0 is odd, so any integer root must also be odd.

Therefore, the sum of these terms will be odd. But this contradicts the fact that the sum of these terms must be even, as we established earlier.

Therefore, the polynomial cannot have any integer roots. So, at most, it can have 0 integer-valued zeros.
