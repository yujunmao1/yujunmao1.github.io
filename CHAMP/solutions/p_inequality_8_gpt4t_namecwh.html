The rearrangement inequality is a fundamental result in combinatorial mathematics that provides a way to compare the sums of products of elements from multiple sequences when the sequences are permuted in different ways. The classical rearrangement inequality typically deals with two sequences, but it can be extended to more than two sequences, which is what you're asking about.

Let's first consider the rearrangement inequality for two sequences. The rearrangement inequality states that for any permutation \( \sigma \) of the indices \( \{1, 2, \ldots, n\} \), the following inequality holds:

\[ a_1b_1 + a_2b_2 + \ldots + a_nb_n \geq a_1b_{\sigma(1)} + a_2b_{\sigma(2)} + \ldots + a_nb_{\sigma(n)} \]
