Along a one-way street there are n parking lots. One-by-one n cars numbered 1 to n enter the street. Each driver i heads to his favorite parking lot a_i, and, if it is free, he occupies it. Otherwise, he continues to the next free lot and occupies it. But if all succeeding lots are occupied, he leaves for good. How many sequences {a_1, ..., a_n} are such that every driver can park, as an expression of n?

You may find the following information useful:

1. If there are n steps of doing a task, and there are p_i ways to do the i-th step, then there are p_1*p_2*...*p_n ways to do the task.
This problem can be solved using the concept of permutations and combinatorics.

Let's consider the first driver. He has n choices of parking lots. Once he has parked, the second driver has n-1 choices, and so on, until the last driver who has only one choice left. 

So, the total number of sequences {a_1, ..., a_n} such that every driver can park is given by the product of the number of choices each driver has, which is n*(n-1)*(n-2)*...*2*1. 

This is the definition of the factorial function, denoted as n!. 

So, the answer to the problem is n!.
