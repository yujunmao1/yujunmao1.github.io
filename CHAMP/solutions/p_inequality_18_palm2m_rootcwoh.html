For positive a, b, c, what is the largest value of sqrt(ab+bc+ac)/(a+b+c)?

You may find the following information useful:

For real numbers a_1≤a_2≤...≤a_n and b_1≤b_2≤...≤b_n, we have a_1*b_1+a_2*b_2...+a_n*b_n≥a_1*c_1+a*2_b*2+...+a_n*c_n≥a_1*b_n+a_2*b_(n-1)+...+a_n*b_1 where (c_1, ..., c_n) is any permutation of (b_1, ..., b_n). More generally, the sum achieves the maximum value when a_i's and b_i's are sorted in the same way, and the minimum value when they are sorted in the opposite way.
Let $a \leq b \leq c$. Then by the AM-GM inequality, we have
\begin{align*}
\sqrt{ab+bc+ac} &\leq \sqrt{3abc} \\
&= \sqrt{3} \sqrt{abc} \\
&\leq \sqrt{3} \sqrt{(a+b+c)(ab+bc+ac)} \\
&= \sqrt{3} \sqrt{(a+b+c)^2 - (a^2+b^2+c^2)} \\
&= \sqrt{3} \sqrt{(a+b+c)^2 - 3(a^2+b^2+c^2) + 3} \\
&= \sqrt{3} \sqrt{(a-b)^2+(b-c)^2+(c-a)^2+3} \\
&\leq \sqrt{3} \sqrt{2(a-b)^2+(b-c)^2+(c-a)^2+3} \\
&= \sqrt{6} \sqrt{(a-b)^2+(b-c)^2+(c-a)^2}.
\end{align*}Equality occurs when $a=b=c$, so the maximum value is $\boxed{\sqrt{6}}$.

The maximum value of $\sqrt{ab+bc+ac}/(a+b+c)$ is $\sqrt{6}$.
