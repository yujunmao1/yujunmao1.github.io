Polynomial division is a mathematical operation that involves dividing a polynomial by another polynomial of the same or lower degree. It is very similar to long division in arithmetic.

The process involves dividing the highest degree term of the dividend (the polynomial you are dividing) by the highest degree term of the divisor (the polynomial you are dividing by), then multiplying the divisor by the result, subtracting that from the dividend, and repeating the process with the remainder.

The result of polynomial division is typically expressed as a quotient plus a remainder, where the degree of the remainder is less than the degree of the divisor. If the degree of the divisor is 1, the process is often referred to as "synthetic division."

For example, if you divide the polynomial x^3 - 3x^2 + 2x - 4 by the polynomial x - 2, the quotient is x^2 - x + 4 and the remainder is -8. So, x^3 - 3x^2 + 2x - 4 = (x - 2)(x^2 - x + 4) - 8.

Polynomial division is used in various areas of mathematics, including algebra, calculus, and number theory.
